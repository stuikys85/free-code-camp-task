Antanas Smetona

One of the most important Lithuanian political figures between World War I and World War II.

The man sitting forth from the right.

Here is a time line of Antanas Smetona life:

  • 1874 10 of August he was born.
  • 1885 was sent to the primary school in Taujėnai, Lithuania.
  • 1893 after graduation, according to his family's wishes, he passed his entrance examinations for the Samogitian Diocesan Seminary in Kaunas.
  • 1897 upon graduation in, Smetona entered the Faculty of Law of the University of Saint Petersburg. He was more interested in history and languages, but knew that as a Catholic his choices were limited to priest, lawyer, or doctor if he wanted to work in Lithuania.
  • 1898 Smetona and his roommate, Vladas Sirutavičius (lt), using a mimeograph printed about 100 copies of a brief Lithuanian grammar written by Petras Avižonis (lt) based on the German-language writings of Frydrichas Kuršaitis.
  • 1902 after his graduation from the University, he moved to Vilnius and worked at the Vilnius Land Bank until 1915. He became an active participant in Lithuanian cultural life.
  • 1904 and 1907, he was on the staff of the Lithuanian newspaper, Vilniaus žinios (The Vilnius News).
  • 1905-1906, he edited the weekly Lietuvos ūkininkas (The Lithuanian Farmer).
  • 1907, Smetona and the Rev. Juozas Tumas-Vaižgantas established a venture to print the newspaper Viltis (The Hope), and started publishing and circulating it.
  • 1914, he started publishing Vairas (The Rudder), a new bi-weekly magazine.
  • 1916, Antanas Smetona, together with other Lithuanians from Vilnius, presented a memorandum to the German Commander-in-Chief of the Eastern Front, in which they demanded the right of the Lithuanian nation to have an independent State.
  • 1917, he started printing the newspaper, Lietuvos Aidas (Lithuania's Echo), working as its publisher and its editor-in-chief. In the first issue of the newspaper, Smetona wrote that the most important goal of the Lithuanian nation was the re-establishment of an independent Lithuanian state.
  • 1917, he participated in the Lithuanian Conference in Vilnius, and was elected Chairman (1917–1919) of the Council of Lithuania (later Council of the State). On 16 February 1918, Antanas Smetona signed the Act of Independence of Lithuania.
  • 1918 and March 1919, he lived primarily in Germany and the Scandinavian countries, soliciting loans for the cause of Lithuanian independence. On 4 April 1919, the State Council of Lithuania elected Smetona the first President of the Republic of Lithuania.
  • 1921 to 1924, he edited several periodicals, including Lietuvos balsas (Voice of Lithuania), Lietuviškas balsas (Lithuanian Voice) and Vairas.
  • 1923, authorities imprisoned Smetona for several days for publishing an article by Augustinas Voldemaras in Vairas. Between 1923 and 1927, he was an assistant Professor at the University of Lithuania - at first in the Chair of Art Theory and History and later at the Department of Philosophy. He lectured on ethics, ancient philosophy and Lithuanian linguistics.
  • 1928, with the approval of the government, he promulgated a new constitution with more extensive presidential powers. In 1929, he removed Voldemaras and assumed dictatorial powers.
  • 1931 and 1938He was re-elected President, both times as the only candidate. The Seimas was not reconvened until 1936 and was composed entirely of Smetona's adherents. In 1938, a third constitution was enacted that retained the general authoritarian character of the 1928 document, and declared that political power in the state was "indivisible." He remained in office until 15 June 1940.
  • 1944 january 9 he died (aged 69) Cleveland, Ohio, U.S.
